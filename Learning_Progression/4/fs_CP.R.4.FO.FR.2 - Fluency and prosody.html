Confirm or correct understanding of a text by using word-attack skills and syntax (i.e., part of speech, position of the word within the sentence) and by using an increasing variety of repair strategies (e.g., slowing reading pace, rereading, and reading on)⚡ Focus Skill

Short Skill Name
Use strategies to confirm / correct understanding
Skill Area
Development of Independence
Domains
Foundations for Reading
Domain Level Expectations
Students begin to identify basic features of print and initiate alphabetic and phonemic awareness skills in order to establish their understanding of foundations of reading.Students extend their understanding of book features and words in print, utilizing a broad range of alphabetic and phonemic awareness skills to begin to accurately decode simple words.Students use established understanding of books and phonemic awareness skills, applying knowledge of simple phonetic patterns and word structure to accurately and rapidly decode an increased number of grade-level words.Students apply their understanding of increasingly complex phonetic patterns and word structures, using strategies to decode two-syllable grade-level words in text.Students expand the scope of their reading; they decode multisyllable words with increased understanding and automaticity, allowing them to identify the purpose for text.Students read a variety of texts, automatically decoding challenging multisyllable words with fluency and appropriate pace and using strategies to clarify comprehension.Students read broadly, fully utilizing their phonetic knowledge, word analysis skills, and fluency strategies to decode challenging multisyllable words in isolation and within a wide variety of texts.
Standards
CP.R.4.FO.FR.2 - Fluency and prosody
Prerequisite Skills
Grade 3 - Confirm or correct understanding of a word or a larger text by using context clues, including both words and text structures, by using phonics, particularly understanding of word morphology, and by slowing reading pace and/or rereadingGrade 3 - Monitor and adjust understanding of text by using background knowledge, predicting, rereading, creating sensory images, generating questions, and applying skills from across the language artsGrade 3 - Use grade-appropriate conversational, general academic, and content-area vocabulary correctly in context, including words that indicate spatial or temporal relationships (e.g., upon, during, beginning)Grade 3 - Use sentence-level context clues (e.g., embedded word definitions, restatement) to determine or clarify the meanings of general academic and content-area words and phrases
ELL Support
Review word-attack strategies and the use of syntax to determine the meanings of unfamiliar words in context.
Content-Area Vocabulary
confirm, correct, syntax, repair strategies
Conceptual Knowledge
knowing to use context clues, phonics skills, and word morphology to correct or confirm comprehension while reading
Linguistic Competencies
setting a purpose for one's own reading; using phonics and knowledge of syntax to infer the meaning of words in context; using repair strategies

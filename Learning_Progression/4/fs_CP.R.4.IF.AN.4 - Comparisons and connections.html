Integrate information from two nonfiction texts on the same topic into a broader understanding in order to write or speak knowledgeably about the topic⚡ Focus Skill

Short Skill Name
Integrate information from texts on a topic
Skill Area
Analysis and Comparison
Domains
Analysis, Evaluation, and Extending Meaning
Domain Level Expectations
Students seek information reflected in different approaches and mediums, using illustrations to understand specific ideas from the text and developing a greater comprehension of the author's points and reasoning.Students engage more fully with informational texts, relating to personal and community experiences and using information extracted from graphics along with ideas from the text and comparing the main ideas of different texts covering the same topic.As students seek content-specific information from different texts, they compare the ideas and details in them by separating each text into the author's claims, evidence, and the logic connecting them.Students thoughtfully draw on multiple perspectives by interpreting and comparing various texts on a topic and integrating information from multiple sources and presentations.When learning about a topic, students select sources and integrate information, and when analyzing media and argument, students connect claims to supporting evidence, understand what makes evidence credible, and identify persuasive strategies.Students interpret and integrate information from a wide range of texts, and they assess evidence and claims, determining whether arguments are convincing, while also considering author's perspective, persuasive techniques, and audience.Deepening their understanding of how authors' perspectives on a topic differ, students connect and assess an argument's logic and evidence, comparing purposes and areas of emphasis and analyzing how an argument is impacted by the medium in which it is presented.Students broaden their analysis of arguments in text to include identifying and explaining counterarguments, bias, false statements, and fallacious reasoning, synthesizing information in order to draw accurate conclusions.Students further analyze the logic and reasoning of arguments presented in a range of historically significant texts, including responses to opposing views and identifying fallacies and use of techniques, such as propoganda, to further the author's purpose.Students evaluate the strengths and weaknesses of evidence presented in an argument, determining the impact of rhetorical devices, bias, and logical fallacies to further an author's purpose and apply this analysis across mediums to synthesize understanding of a range of perspectives in text.Students use a critical lens to interpret texts and synthesize information from different sources and presentations. They evaluate an argument's quality, analyzing use of evidence and support, rhetorical devices, and fallacies, and they analyze media's influence.Students compare the development, purpose, and style of complex texts and evaluate complex arguments and media messages, dismantling faulty logic, uncovering the author's values and unstated assumptions, and evaluating the impact of rhetoric, images, and logical fallacies.
Standards
CP.R.4.IF.AN.4 - Comparisons and connections
Prerequisite Skills
Grade 3 - Use prior knowledge and evidence from the text to draw simple conclusions about information or eventsGrade 3 - Describe the key details of an informational text and explain how they support the main ideaGrade 3 - Compare and contrast the most important points and key details presented in informational texts on the same topicGrade 4 - Explain the explicit meaning of an informational text based on evidence from the text (e.g., locate details and examples that explain an idea in the text)Grade 4 - Summarize a short informational passage including the main idea and key detailsGrade 4 - Interpret information presented visually, orally, or quantitatively (e.g., in charts, graphs, diagrams, timelines, animations, or interactive online text features) and explain how the information contributes to deeper understanding of the text's topic
ELL Support
Model using a Venn diagram to compare informational texts and then have students try. Provide sentence stems to write or speak about the topic.
Content-Area Vocabulary
information, topic, similarities, differences
Conceptual Knowledge
recognizing how information from one text compares to information from another text on the same topic; recognizing how particular pieces of information apply to understanding of a topic
Linguistic Competencies
using simple compound sentences that require simple conjunctions

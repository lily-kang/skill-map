Short Skill Name
Find rhyme / alliteration / rhythm in oral language
Skill Area
Author's Word Choice and Figurative Language
Domains
Structure, Genre, and Author's Craft
Domain Level Expectations
As a foundation for early reading comprehension, with support, students begin to recognize common types and formats of text, understanding that a text has an author and illustrator, and a beginning and an end.Through early exposure to different genres, students become aware of sensory language and sound devices, and develop an understanding of simple text features and structures.By exploring texts, students begin to recognize the general structural differences between fiction and nonfiction. They are exposed to sound devices, and playful and figurative language to develop basic understanding of how word choice affects text.Students identify patterns and imagery as they build vocabulary, understanding both literal and figurative language. They describe the differences between genres and explore basic text structures, becoming more aware of how these elements influence text. Students understand that words can create images and communicate a mood or tone. They describe genre characteristics and recognize parts of books, plays, and poems to help determine common forms and organization of text. By broadening their knowledge of author's word choice, structural elements, and specific terms for literary forms, students are able to comprehend and discuss characteristics of a variety of texts, including origin myths and traditional literature.Students determine the meaning of figurative language and analyze the contributions of sensory detail and literary devices. To further develop reading comprehension, they compare structures and identify terminology for the parts of different literary texts.In order to understand the effects of author's word choice, students determine connotative meaning and examine figurative language, sensory detail, and common literary devices. They expand their knowledge of different forms of literary text, including traditional stories, by analyzing common elements and organization.As students deepen their understanding of the author's craft and the underlying meaning of a text, they analyze how language choices, structure and form, and common archetypes and allusions all affect the meaning and impact of a literary work.Students analyze an author's use of language, structural choices, and genre characteristics in order to understand their impact on meaning and contributions to the text's development and style. Continuing to analyze word choice and structure through exposure to a variety of genres, students deepen their knowledge of the effects of these elements on text meaning, mood, and tone in order to expand their comprehension of increasingly sophisticated texts. To interpret more complex works, students continue to analyze genre characteristics and structural choices, and their consideration of craft and structure broadens to include the cumulative impact of an author's word choice on a text. Students apply increased understanding of the aesthetic impact of author's language choices and sophisticated structural choices as they analyze and evaluate the overall meaning of more complicated and diverse texts.To develop skills of literary criticism, students examine the aesthetic impact of word choices and literary techniques, analyzing and comparing how genre characteristics, structural choices, and devices such as irony are used to create the meaning and tone in a broad array of complex literary works.

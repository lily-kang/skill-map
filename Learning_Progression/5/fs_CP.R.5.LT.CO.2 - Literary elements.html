They use key details to retell stories and describe a stated moral, gaining a firmer sense of the basic elements of stories.Students recount a text's key ideas, details, and central message as a foundation for interpreting literary elements and for reflecting on and drawing simple conclusions about the meaning of stories, fables, and folktales from diverse cultures.Students use strategies such as rereading, predicting, and background knowledge to comprehend texts. They interpret literary elements and important details in texts to answer their questions and deepen understanding of the meaning of literary texts from around the world.Students begin to summarize stories and rely on context clues to understand literary texts. They use close reading and textual evidence when making inferences in order to better understand literary elements and their contribution to texts from different genres.Students apply increasingly sophisticated reading strategies to comprehend texts, accurately drawing from textual evidence. They compare and contrast how literary elements are used to create more complex themes as a foundation for deeper literary analysis in later grades. Students continue to use various strategies to understand literary texts across genres, while analyzing connections among story elements, summarizing and drawing conclusions that deepen their knowledge of literature.Students read widely and automatically correct their understanding of a text. Standards
CP.R.5.LT.CO.2 - Literary elements
Prerequisite Skills
Grade 3 - Identify the narrator in a story or poem told from the first person point of viewGrade 4 - Explain the difference between first- and third-person narration (e.g., recognize whether or not the narrator is a character in the story), and compare and contrast the narrative points of view of different stories
ELL Support
Present a literary text with first person narration. Use language models to discuss with students what they know about the narrator or other characters. Discuss what would be different in a third-person narration. Have students rewrite the text in third-person.
Content-Area Vocabulary
narrator, speaker, point of view, first person, third person
Conceptual Knowledge
understanding how narrative point of view affects a story or poem
Linguistic Competencies
using varied sentence structures, verb forms, specific academic vocabulary, descriptive language, adjectives, and adverbs

Short Skill Name

Skill Area
Point of View
Domains

Domain Level Expectations
With support, students begin to engage with simple literary texts read aloud by asking and answering questions and retelling details using illustrations, establishing their understanding of story elements and gaining new vocabulary.Students listen to stories for key details, allowing them to retell and identify essential story elements and make predictions as a foundation for close reading of text.Students begin to read literary texts independently and make connections to ideas through personal experience and prior knowledge. They use key details to retell stories and describe a stated moral, gaining a firmer sense of the basic elements of stories.Students recount a text's key ideas, details, and central message as a foundation for interpreting literary elements and for reflecting on and drawing simple conclusions about the meaning of stories, fables, and folktales from diverse cultures.Students use strategies such as rereading, predicting, and background knowledge to comprehend texts. They interpret literary elements and important details in texts to answer their questions and deepen understanding of the meaning of literary texts from around the world.Students begin to summarize stories and rely on context clues to understand literary texts. They use close reading and textual evidence when making inferences in order to better understand literary elements and their contribution to texts from different genres.Students apply increasingly sophisticated reading strategies to comprehend texts, accurately drawing from textual evidence. They compare and contrast how literary elements are used to create more complex themes as a foundation for deeper literary analysis in later grades. Students continue to use various strategies to understand literary texts across genres, while analyzing connections among story elements, summarizing and drawing conclusions that deepen their knowledge of literature.Students read widely and automatically correct their understanding of a text. They analyze the influence of literary elements with increased sophistication, drawing conclusions and interpreting complex literary texts in order to develop summaries that accurately reflect the text's overall meaning.Students make genuine connections to literary texts, and they deepen their understanding of how related narrative elements and themes develop, weighing evidence in order to make a comprehensive interpretation of texts.Students draw larger conclusions from literary texts, connecting passages to the overall meaning and analyzing relationships between complex textual elements and ways they contribute to significant ideas and themes.Students implement more advanced reading strategies, analyzing how literary elements shape the narrative and can refine the meaning and significance of literary text. They infer themes and draw conclusions based on a broad spectrum of increasingly sophisticated textual evidence.Students readily apply close reading strategies to analyze the interaction of narrative elements and their contribution to a literary text's deeper meaning. Making inferences and considering multiple interpretations and cultural contexts in a wide array of genres, they prepare to synthesize ideas in more complex texts in Grade 12.Students automatically utilize a full array of strategies when approaching challenging literary texts, synthesizing complex concepts in order to interpret ambiguous themes. They infer meaning while considering multiple interpretations of text, including cutural and intellectual influences.
Standards
CP.R.8.LT.CO.1 - Interaction of literary elements
Prerequisite Skills
Grade 7 - Identify the point of view (e.g., first/third person, limited/omniscient, subjective/objective)Grade 7 - Analyze how the author develops and contrasts different characters or narrators within the text (e.g., how two characters may see a situation differently)Grade 7 - Analyze how point of view influences the way the story is told, and how the author develops the narrator (e.g., how Robert Cormier slowly reveals the narrator's reality in I Am the Cheese)
ELL Support
Present two or three brief excerpts of literary text. Use a compare/contrast graphic organizer to categorize information about the point of view in each and the impact of point of view on plot or reader perception. Model analysis of one excerpt for students.
Content-Area Vocabulary
narrative point of view, narrator, speaker, first person, third person, third-person limited, omniscient, subjective, objective, perception
Conceptual Knowledge
understanding the effects of narrative point of view on a literary text and on the reader's perception; recognizing different points of view
Linguistic Competencies
using varied sentence structures, verb forms, specific academic vocabulary, adjectives, conjunctions, comparatives, and superlatives

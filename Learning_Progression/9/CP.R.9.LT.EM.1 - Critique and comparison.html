Compare and contrast how the same subject, theme, key scene, or story is treated in different mediums (e.g., describe the differences between how a story from mythology is told in an epic poem and represented in a classical painting)

Short Skill Name
Compare effect of varied mediums on same subject
Skill Area
Modes of Representation
Domains
Extending Meaning and Deepening Understanding
Domain Level Expectations
Students find similarities between different versions (audio, video, live version) of a printed text, and with support, make comparisons between characters and events in stories as a foundation for determining differences in literary texts independently. Students draw from both words and illustrations to describe literary elements and to make comparisons within and between texts, in order to gain deeper understanding of what is read.Students continue to draw from both the words and illustrations of print or digital texts, including similar versions of a story, for greater understanding and the ability to make personal connections and comparisons between texts across cultures.Students compare traditional tales to deepen understanding of literary elements and common themes, and understand how illustrations contribute to a story as a foundation for interpreting visual and multimedia elements in later grades.Students interpret an oral or visual presentation of a text to confirm their understanding, and they make inferences and relevant comparisons between works of traditional literature across cultures in order to understand how similar themes are used in different texts.Students explore the contributions of visual and multimedia elements to a text; they make comparisons between stories in similar genres to deepen understading and in preparation for comparing different perspectives in literary texts to their own.Students make comparisons between written texts and live or multimedia versions, exploring how literature in various forms is perceived, and they continue to make connections between texts and personal experience and compare genres to deepen understanding of similar literary themes.Students analyze how the techniques used in each literary medium (film, live, recorded, multimedia) affect the piece, and they make comparisons between fictional and nonfictional accounts of a historical period to deepen their understanding of literature.Students analyze the extent to which techniques used in each literary medium (film, live, recorded, multimedia) affect the piece; they compare modern works of fiction with similarly themed traditional stories in order to determine how the texts stay true to source material.Students apply content-specific knowledge (language arts, history, social studies) to make critical comparisons between texts; they analyze how a specific subject or scene is treated in two different artistic mediums, as well as how modern works draw from traditional source material in order to gain greater understanding of the meaning of various story treatments.Students analyze with greater sophistication how a specific subject or scene is treated in different artistic mediums, as well as how authors draw from traditional, influential source material in modern works in order to gain greater insight into the meaning of literary texts.Students analyze multiple versions of increasingly complex literary texts to evaluate how each version interprets the source material; they compare ideas and themes in texts in order to understand how literary works reflect the time period in which they were written.Students critique complex literary works, considering genre and author's intent or philosophy as well as literary elements, structure, and levels of theme and meaning. Students study multiple versions of a text as well as secondary sources. They consider a text's social and historical context and make insightful comparisons.

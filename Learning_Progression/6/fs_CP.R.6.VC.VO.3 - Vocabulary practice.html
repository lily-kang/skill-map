Skill Area
Vocabulary in Context
Domains
Vocabulary Development
Domain Level Expectations
Students begin to demonstrate understanding of age-appropriate vocabulary, including functional and organizational language, applying it in both academic and everyday settings.Students explore word relationships and use a range of strategies to determine the meaning of unknown words as they build and apply core vocabulary in conversing, reading, and responding to texts.Students use several strategies to develop greater understanding of word meanings and relationships, including categorizing and applying knowledge of word roots and affixes, to practice using core vocabulary in academic and daily life.Students apply multiple strategies when encountering unknown words, including using word morphology and beginning dictionaries, in order to increase their core vocabulary, understand word relationships, and strengthen real-life connections to words.Students apply strategies to determine the meanings of words, including nonliteral meaning, in grade-level texts to expand their academic and content-area vocabulary.Students continue to use context, reference materials, and other strategies to determine word meanings and demonstrate understanding of figurative language in order to precisely select words that express actions, emotions, and states of being.Students begin to describe the feelings or images derived from words with similar dictionary definitions and continue to acquire new vocabulary in course content in order to accurately use appropriate domain-specific words when discussing a variety of subjects.Students' knowledge of vocabulary expands to include analogies and the connotative meanings of words and phrases, and words important to comprehension and expression in order to choose correct, precise language when discussing particular subjects.Students apply strategies with greater automaticity to clarify unfamiliar word meanings, including those of figurative language and connotation, in order to use vocabulary correctly and better comprehend literary and informational texts.Students intuitively use multiple strategies to determine word meanings, acquiring knowledge of increasingly sophisticated word relationships and figurative language in order to choose appropriate language and gain deeper comprehension of texts.Students apply multiple strategies to verify word meanings, including the discernment of connotative or nuanced differences, in order to gain independence in use of vocabulary for reading, discussing, and writing about complex texts.Students independently apply multiple strategies to verify word meanings and to analyze sophisticated word relationships in order to expand their understanding and use of vocabulary needed for reading and communicating about complex texts.Students strategize and expand their knowledge of vocabulary by focusing on words that are key to comprehension of complex texts, authors' word choices, and figurative language; they use this knowledge in the precise application of language when reading, writing, speaking, and listening.Students automatically apply multiple strategies flexibly and independently, using a sophisticated vocabulary and understanding of language for reading, discussing, and communicating about college-level texts.

Prerequisite Skills
Grade 5 - Use context clues (e.g., cause/effect, comparison, word relationships) to determine or clarify the meanings of general academic and content-area words and phrasesGrade 5 - Use a range of strategies to determine or clarify the meanings of general academic words and phrases in grade-appropriate literary texts, including cause-and-effect relationships, comparisons, Latin and Greek roots, and reference materialsGrade 5 - Use a range of strategies to determine or clarify the meanings of general academic and content-area specific words and phrases in grade-appropriate informational texts, including cause-and-effect relationships, comparisons, Latin and Greek roots, and reference materialsGrade 5 - Use grade-appropriate general academic vocabulary, including transition and connective words (e.g., however, although, nevertheless, in addition) correctly in context as well as grade-appropriate content-area vocabulary when working within specific subject areas (e.g., chemical, congress, liberty, plot)
ELL Support
Provide sentence stems to use the target vocabulary, based on the content being studied (e.g., The Louisiana Purchase involved a contract in which _____.). Have students create a graphic organizer in which the word is defined, illustrated, and used in context.
